## Known gaps (honest limits)

- Epcoritamab monotherapy phase 3 EPCORE DLBCL-1 (confirmatory trial): topline press-release only as of Jul 2026 — no peer-reviewed publication, no PMID/DOI. OS not yet reported. Update when a full paper/meeting presentation appears.
- Copanlisib withdrawal: regulatory/press-release only — no PMID/DOI. Best citable source is the Bayer press release; also note the rubric correction (failed trial = CHRONOS-4, not CHRONOS-3).
- No dedicated citation for the explicit claim that R-CHOP-backbone components and cisplatin do not reach cytotoxic CNS levels — Korfel 2015 is PCNSL-framed and does not headline this contrast. Needs a dedicated secondary-CNS-prophylaxis/pharmacology reference to ground the R-ICE-vs-R-DHAP CNS-penetration argument.
- No study directly quantifies the incremental/cumulative peripheral neuropathy from prior vincristine (CHOP/R-CHOP) followed by polatuzumab vedotin (MMAE) — the vincristine->pola stacking concern is mechanistic (shared microtubule-inhibitor MoA) plus the FDA POLIVY label, not a head-to-head study.
- FDA POLIVY Prescribing Information and the STARGLO FDA CRL (2025-07-18) are regulatory documents without PMIDs/DOIs; they support label/regulatory framing but are not fetchable as papers.
- cns-pharmacology-korfel-2015 has a DOI but no cleanly linked PMID/PMCID (Expert Rev Hematol indexing gap) — fetchable by DOI only; verify retrieval.
- CAR-T in secondary CNS lymphoma is grounded primarily by a meta-analysis (Shawabkeh 2026), a narrative review (Houillier 2024), and a single case report (Takigawa 2024) — no dedicated prospective/registry trial of CAR-T specifically in secondary CNS DLBCL; evidence base is inherently non-randomized.
