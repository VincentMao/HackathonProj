## Clinical note (SOAP)

**Assessment:** 86-year-old woman with primary refractory DLBCL (GCB, BCL2+/BCL6-/MYC- by IHC), spleen-dominant stage III, progressing on imaging about four months after age-attenuated mini-R-CHOP. Repeat biopsy equivocal (necrosis, no clonal population), but serial PET and clinical picture are consistent with refractory disease. ECOG 1 with robust functional status (independent, physically active).

**Plan / reasoning:**
- Not a CAR T-cell therapy candidate given age and geographic distance from the cellular-therapy center; the logistics and expected toxicity outweigh benefit here.
- Tafasitamab-lenalidomide is not appropriate in primary-refractory disease and was excluded.
- Options discussed with the patient and the referring oncologist: polatuzumab + mosunetuzumab (lower intensity) versus epcoritamab + GemOx (higher intensity). Given her robust performance status and explicit goals, chose **epcoritamab + GemOx** to avoid under-treatment. This choice is acknowledged to diverge from strict guideline defaults for her age; rationale and patient agreement documented.
- Bispecific step-up dosing to be delivered at this center (cycle 1), with transfer of ongoing therapy to the local oncologist for proximity.
- Supportive care: acyclovir and trimethoprim-sulfamethoxazole prophylaxis, antiemetics, dexamethasone per step-up protocol.
- Baseline CBC, CMP, LDH, hepatitis B/C and HIV serologies today. Restaging PET/CT after 3 cycles. Close coordination with the referring oncologist.
